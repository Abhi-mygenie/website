Tone on Petpooja — 2 Styles Applied to Real Copy

Same facts, different delivery. I've applied both tones to 4 real sections of the page so you can feel the difference.

Style A — Fair & Factual

No edge · Builds trust · Credibility-first
Petpooja intro
"Petpooja built India's most popular restaurant POS over 14 years and 1.5 lakh outlets. That's a real track record. Here's what's changed in the market since then."
KDS comparison
"KDS is available on Petpooja as a marketplace add-on. On MyGenie, it's included from the Growth plan."
AI section intro
"Petpooja focuses on core billing and operations. MyGenie adds a layer of practical AI — upsell suggestions, automatic report audits, customer segmentation — built directly into the Pro plan."
Pricing section
"Petpooja's India pricing isn't listed on their website — you'll need to call for a quote. MyGenie's plans are ₹799, ₹1,499 and ₹2,499 per outlet per month, billed annually. No surprises."
When Fair works best: If your audience is mid-journey (still evaluating, not yet frustrated with Petpooja). Honesty = credibility. They'll trust the positive claims more because you didn't bash the competitor.

Style B — Direct & Pointed

More edge · Speaks to frustration · Switcher-friendly
Petpooja intro
"Petpooja built a solid product. But it was built in a different era — before AI, before mobile-first, before restaurant owners expected to see their profit per table in real time."
KDS comparison
"Petpooja charges extra for KDS. So does it for the Captain App and Loyalty. On MyGenie, these aren't extras — they're in the plan."
AI section intro
"Petpooja gives you reports. MyGenie gives you answers. The AI reads every report, flags every anomaly, and tells you what to fix — automatically."
Pricing section
"Petpooja doesn't publish its India pricing. You have to call. We publish ours: ₹799 / ₹1,499 / ₹2,499 per outlet per month. If a company won't show you the price upfront, ask yourself why."
When Pointed works best: If your audience is already frustrated with Petpooja (support issues, add-on pricing surprises). Speaks directly to their pain. Risk: can feel aggressive to a casual browser. Recommend using pointed copy only in sections 3–6, after you've built credibility in sections 1–2.

How the A/B tracking works (zero extra tools)

1
Page loads → randomly picks A or B (50/50). Stored in localStorage
2
Same visitor always sees same variant. Consistent experience across sessions.
3
GTM receives ab_variant: "hero_a" in dataLayer on page view.
4
GA4 custom dimension shows demo bookings split by variant. Winner in 2–3 weeks.
